An Explosion of Flavors

Indonesian cuisine varies greatly by region and has many different influences. It often demonstrates complex flavour, acquired from certain ingredients and bumbu spices mixture.

Indonesian dishes have rich flavours; most often described as savory, hot and spicy, and also combination of basic tastes such as sweet, salty, sour and bitter.

Jakarta

Indonesia’s capital city, Jakarta is a historic mix of cultures from Javanese, Malay, Chinese, Arab, Indian and European.

Yogyakarta

Bali

Nasi Kuning

Nasi kuning, or sometimes called nasi kunyit, is an Indonesian fragrant rice dish cooked with coconut milk and turmeric, hence the name nasi kuning.

Nasi Gudeg

Gudeg is a traditional Javanese cuisine from Yogyakarta and Central Java, Indonesia. Gudeg is made from young unripe jack fruit stewed for several hours with palm sugar, and coconut milk.
